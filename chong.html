Dr. Trisha Chong

Department of Pathology, Stanford University

"Microbial Nitrogen Fixation: Innovation Through Evolution and Engineering"

Abstract

Trisha Chong grew up in Honolulu, Hawaii. She first came to California to pursue her undergraduate education at Claremont Mckenna College, where she studied the effects of the male-kililng bacterium, Spiroplasma, on fruit fly development in the lab of Patrick Ferree. After a short period working in a biological chemistry lab at UCLA, she started her PhD at Stanford University. Under the leadership of Lucy Shapiro, she uncovered the role of a critical kinase in timing the cell cycle of the asymmetrically dividing bacterium, Caulobacter crescentus. Trisha stayed at Stanford University for her postdoc in Ellen Yeh's lab, where she is currently studying the coordination between photosynthesis and nitrogen fixation in aquatic microbes.
