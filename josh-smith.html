Dr. Josh Smith

University of California, Santa Cruz

"An unintended ecological experiment: lessons on resilience from a kelp forest ecosystem in transition"

Abstract

Ecological communities can persist in stable configurations across generations or rapidly transform into structurally and functionally distinct states. My research program investigates how biological and environmental contexts shape these processes in coastal and marine ecosystems by exploring the drivers of community regulation, persistence, and transition dynamics. First, I will describe how the resistance of a foundation species to an unexpected marine heatwave, a catastrophic sea star die-off, and an outbreak of purple sea urchins, revealed underlying mechanisms of ecological community and ecosystem persistence. I will then share how this ecosystem in transition illuminated the importance of trait-mediated responses in shaping community regulation, from sea urchins to sea otters. Collectively, my research group combines observational and experimental approaches, data science, and syntheses to address hypotheses concerning ecological responses to environmental change, and to advance conservation strategies that benefit people and nature.
