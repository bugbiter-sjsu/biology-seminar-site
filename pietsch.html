Dr. Carlie Pietsch

San Jose State University

What can marine invertebrates tell us about surviving a global catastrophe?

Abstract

The stratigraphic record preserved in the shallow marine environment of the U.S. Gulf Coastal Plain (GCP) records the end-Cretaceous mass extinction event. These sections, which cover the late Maastrichtian (last 400ky of the Cretaceous) through the Cretaceous-Paleogene Boundary (KPB) and into the early Danian (first 1 to 2 My post-impact), allow us to study the ecological responses to two major stresses: the long-term warming from the Deccan volcanic activity and the devastating environmental changes caused by the bolide impact. Using field-based collections from three key KPB sites, we apply D47 clumped isotope paleothermometry, quantitative diversity, size, and metabolism metrics paired with functional ecology to understand the strategies shallow marine life used to recover.
